PAKISTAN ZINDABAD

Today marks the 77th anniversary of Pakistan's independence.This day honors the moment when Pakistan gained independence from the United Kingdom, officially becoming a sovereign nation after the end of British rule.

Founders Of Pakistan

QUAID E AZAM

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah was the key figure behind Pakistan's independence. Initially a member of the Indian National Congress, he later led the All India Muslim League, advocating for a separate nation for Muslims. His leadership and strategic negotiations were crucial in gaining international support for the creation of Pakistan. Despite significant challenges, Jinnah's vision and determination led to the establishment of Pakistan as an independent state on 14 August 1947. His contributions remain a cornerstone of the nation's history.

ALLAMA IQBAL

Allama Muhammad Iqbal, known as the "Spiritual Father of Pakistan," played a crucial role in the independence movement through his visionary ideas. In his 1930 Allahabad Address, Iqbal proposed the creation of a separate Muslim state, laying the ideological foundation for Pakistan. His poetry and writings inspired Muslims to pursue unity, self-awareness, and independence.
Iqbal also encouraged Muhammad Ali Jinnah to lead the Muslim League, influencing the path to Pakistan's creation.

FATIMAH JINNAH

Fatimah Jinnah, known as the "Mother of the Nation," played a crucial role in Pakistan's independence. As the sister and close advisor to Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah, she supported him throughout the struggle.
She mobilized women, encouraging their participation in the Pakistan Movement, and was a symbol of resilience and unity. After independence, she continued to advocate for democracy, social justice, and women's rights, remaining an influential figure in Pakistan's history.

HERITAGE OF PAKISTAN

The national bird of Pakistan is Chukar partridge. They are commonly known as Chakor and it is a Eurasian upland game bird in the pheasant family Phasianidae.

Field hockey is the national sport, and was popular for several decades, with some of Pakistan's greatest sporting accomplishments having taken place in this sport, along with squash.

Pakistan flag consists of dark green with a white vertical stripe, a white crescent and a five pronged star in the middle. The green color represents Islam and the majority of Muslims in Pakistan; the white stripe represents religious minorities living side by side with Muslims
